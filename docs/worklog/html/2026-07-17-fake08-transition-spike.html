Chasing the room-transition spike

2026-07-17 · and it’s compute, not GC

Steady Celeste runs ~110 fps on the S3, but every room load costs a single ~95 ms frame. Isolated the spike frames (heap-instrumented MEASURE_FPS): they’re Step(), and they allocate ~54 KB — Celeste’s load_room spawning a room’s objects. Then a decisive test: made z8lua’s GC lazy and re-measured. The load_room spikes didn’t budge → the cost is inherent interpreter compute, not GC. No cheap fix.

The spike frames

drawFrame stays a flat 3.6 ms, so the ~95 ms is entirely the Lua VM. Heap deltas on spikes:

framestepheap Δreading
f0246 ms−289 KBboot / _init (one-time)
f175104 ms+12 KB freeda GC-dominant frame
f38795 ms−54 KB allocload_room — spawns objects
f61195 ms−54 KB allocload_room — spawns objects

z8lua’s incremental GC does collection work proportional to that 54 KB burst (default GCMUL=200 → ~2×, concentrated in the same frame). So: load_room logic, or GC?

The decisive experiment

Tuned our z8lua fork’s GC lazy — GCPAUSE 200→400, GCMUL 200→80 (no fake-08 source/ touched) — and re-measured the same spikes:

spikebaselinelazy GCverdict
f175 — GC-dominant (+12 KB)104 ms77 msGC-bound → helped
f387 — load_room (−54 KB)95 ms96 msunchanged
f611 — load_room (−54 KB)95 ms96 msunchanged
The room-load spikes are inherent interpreter compute, not GC. Halving the GC rate left them untouched; it only sped a GC-dominant frame. GC tuning does not fix the transition hitch.

Implication

There’s no cheap separate fix for the transition spike — it’s the same z8lua-interpreter-speed limit, concentrated on the heaviest (room-load) frame. It’s acceptable as-is: steady play ~110 fps, room transitions carry a screen-wipe that masks a single dropped frame, and transitions are infrequent in real play. Bytecode precompile would not help (it speeds cart parse, not load_room).

So the perf roadmap simplifies: don’t chase GC or a bespoke transition fix. The only lever is general z8lua speed, worth pursuing only if a hitch-free 60 fps becomes a hard requirement — profile first.

Landed / reverted

  • Kept: the MEASURE_FPS heap + spike-isolation instrumentation (a better perf tool).
  • Reverted: the z8lua GC tuning (didn’t fix the real spike) and the temp scanInput auto-drive.
